Ahmad Yasawi

About Ahmad Yasawi

Life and career

Ahmad Yasawi (1093–1166) — the greatest Sufi poet and spiritual leader of the Turkic world. This revered figure from Yasi (present-day Türkistan, Kazakhstan) is the founder of the spiritual movement that spread Islam among the Turkic peoples of Central Asia. His collection of poems known as Divan-i Hikmet is the first great example of Sufi literature in the Turkic language — written in plain, accessible Turkic, these verses spread from heart to heart.

Scholarly and creative work

Ahmad Yasawi was born in 1093 in the city of Yasi (present-day Türkistan, Kazakhstan). Having lost his father at an early age, he travelled to Bukhara to study under Yusuf Hamadani, one of the great Sufi masters of his time. After his master's death, he returned to Yasi, where he established a large spiritual community.

When Yasawi reached the age of 63 — the age at which the Prophet passed away — he declared himself unworthy of living among the people and retreated to an underground cell, a form of subterranean hermitage. He spent the last thirty years of his life there, yet never severed his connection with the people.

Contribution to society
