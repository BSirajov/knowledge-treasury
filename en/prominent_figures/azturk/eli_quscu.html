About Ali Qushji

Life and career

Ali Qushji is a distinguished figure of Turkic / Uzbek-Ottoman heritage, widely recognized in the field of Astronomy & Mathematics. This figure's life and work occupy an important place in the scientific, literary, and cultural memory of the Turkic world.

Scholarly and creative work

Ali Qushji is presented as a figure who contributed to the established traditions of this field and left an enduring mark on both national and broader Turkic cultural memory.

Among the principal areas of this work, particular note is given to activities in Astronomy & Mathematics; contributions to Enlightenment thought, culture, and scholarly heritage; and to the role in the shared memory of the Turkic world. These examples are the primary indicators of the scholarly, literary, or cultural influence generated in this field.

Contribution to society

Ali Gushcu's activity increased the intergeographic circulation of scientific knowledge in the Turkish-Islamic world.

Key works and contributions

  • 1
    Treatise on fi'l-Heya
    Systematizing the theoretical foundations of astronomy, the classic Muslim scientific tradition of celestial mechanics is presented at a high level.
  • 2
    Muhammadiyah fi'l-Hisab
    This work, which explains mathematical calculation methods in a didactic form, has been one of the main sources in madrasa education for a long time.
  • 3
    Zic-i Ulugbey comments
    Samarkand developed a tradition of scientific interpretation that conveyed the observation and calculation results of the school of astronomy to a wide audience.

Notable aspects of the life and work

🔭 Scientific bridge from Samarkand to Istanbul
He accelerated the transmission of mathematics and astronomy by bringing the scientific knowledge of the Ulugbey school to the Ottoman environment.
🏫 He taught at the Hagia Sophia madrasa
Teaching activities in Istanbul played a key role in the integration of classical exact sciences into Ottoman education.
