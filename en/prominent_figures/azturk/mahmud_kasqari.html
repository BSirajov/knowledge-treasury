Mahmud al-Kashgari

1005 – 1102

Karakhanid / Uyghur Azerbaijani & Turkic heritage

About Mahmud al-Kashgari

Life and career

Mahmud al-Kashgari (1005–1102) — author of Diwan Lughat al-Turk, the first great lexical encyclopedia of the Turkic languages. Regarded as the founder of Turkological scholarship, this great scholar personally investigated all Turkic dialects and immortalized their vocabulary, grammar, poetry, and proverbs. The world map included in his work is one of the earliest maps to place the Turkic world at its centre.

Scholarly and creative work

Mahmud al-Kashgari was a linguist, geographer, and encyclopedist of Karakhanid Turkic origin. He completed Diwan Lughat al-Turk (The Compendium of Turkic Languages) in Baghdad between 1072 and 1074, dedicating it to the Abbasid Caliph al-Muqtadi Billah. His aim was to introduce the Turkic language to the Baghdad court and teach Arabic speakers Turkic.

For this purpose, he first travelled all the Turkic lands — personally visiting every region inhabited by Turkic tribes, from Kashgar onwards, to collect linguistic specimens. The collected material is so rich that many Turkic dialects no longer extant today can only be known through Kashgari's work.

Contribution to society

"I have studied the Turkic languages ​​thoroughly and distinguished their correct forms from incorrect ones. I learned the languages ​​of all Turkic, Turkmen, Oghuz, Chigil, Yaghma, and Kyrgyz tribes." — From Kashgari's preface
