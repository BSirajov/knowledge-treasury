Togolok Moldo About

Life and career

Togolok Moldo (1860–1942) was a Kyrgyz intellectual, poet, and educator. During the transition from the nineteenth to the twentieth century he made major contributions to written literature and public education in Kyrgyz.

Scholarly and creative work

Through poetry, epics, and educational writings Moldo strengthened the cultural status of the Kyrgyz language and helped form village schools and a reading public.

Contribution to society

Togolok Moldo is a founder of Kyrgyz national education; his work laid groundwork for later literature, journalism, and pedagogy.
