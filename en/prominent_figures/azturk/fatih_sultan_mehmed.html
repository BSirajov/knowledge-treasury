Mehmed II (Fatih Sultan Mehmed) About

Life and career

Mehmed II (1432–1481), known as Fatih Sultan Mehmed, was an Ottoman sultan. After the conquest of Constantinople in 1453 he received the title Fatih (Conqueror) and turned the Ottoman Empire into a global power.

Scholarly and creative work

Mehmed combined military strategy, governance, and cultural policy, promoting the restoration of conquered cities and support for learning. His reign is considered the beginning of the Ottoman renaissance.

Contribution to society

The conquest of Constantinople is a turning point in world history; Mehmed is one of the central symbols of Ottoman-Turkish political and cultural heritage.

Key works and contributions
