Kamoliddin Bekhzod About

Life and career

Kamoliddin Bekhzod (1455–1535) was the greatest miniaturist of the Herat and Bukhara schools, representing the peak of Timurid visual art.

Scholarly and creative work

Bekhzod created illustrations for works such as those of Nasir al-Din Tusi and Sa'di of Shiraz, bringing new expression to Persian miniature art.

Contribution to society

His school influenced later miniaturists and helped Central Asian art enter the canon of world cultural heritage.
