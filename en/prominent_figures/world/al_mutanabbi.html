Al-Mutanabbi About

Life and career

Abu al-Tayyib Ahmad ibn al-Husayn al-Mutanabbi (915–965) is one of the greatest classics of Arabic poetry. He is known for powerful, epic, and philosophical lyric.

Scholarly and creative work

Al-Mutanabbi wrote powerful qasidas praising rulers and treating themes of conquest and wisdom, raising the poetic potential of Arabic to its peak.

Contribution to society

Al-Mutanabbi is called the 'poet king' of Arabic poetry; his verses have been remembered for millennia and remain integral to educational curricula.

Key works and contributions

  • 1
    Qasidas (Diwan)
    Classic qasidas on praise, conquest, and wisdom.
  • 2
    Epic lyric
    Powerful, philosophical, and epic poems.
  • 3
    Arabic poetic classic
    The best-known and most repeated verses of Arabic poetry.
