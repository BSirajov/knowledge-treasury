Al-Ghazali About

Life and career

Abu Hamid Muhammad al-Ghazali (1058–1111) was an Islamic scholar, theologian, and mystic born in Tus. He wrote fundamental works in philosophy, law, and Sufism.

Scholarly and creative work

Al-Ghazali deeply influenced Islamic thought through works such as Ihya Ulum al-Din (Revival of the Religious Sciences) and Tahafut al-Falasifa (The Incoherence of the Philosophers).

Contribution to society

Al-Ghazali is among the classics of Islamic theology and mystical thought; his ideas have had wide influence from East to West.

Key works and contributions

  • 1
    Ihya Ulum al-Din (Revival of the Religious Sciences)
    Fundamental treatise on Islamic sciences, ethics, and mysticism.
  • 2
    Tahafut al-Falasifa (Incoherence of the Philosophers)
    Critical debate between philosophy and theology.
  • 3
    Sufi treatises
    Writings on spiritual perfection and mystical experience.
