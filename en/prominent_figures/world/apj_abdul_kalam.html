A. P. J. Abdul Kalam About

Life and career

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian missile scientist and 11th President of India, known as the Missile Man.

Scholarly and creative work

Kalam played a leading role in ISRO and DRDO missile technology, Pokhran nuclear tests, and satellite programs, contributing to youth science education.

Contribution to society

He is a symbol of India's modern scientific-technological independence and an inspirational figure for youth.

Key works and contributions

  • 1
    SLV-III
    India's first satellite launch vehicle.
  • 2
    Pokhran-II
    1998 nuclear tests.
  • 3
    Wings of Fire
    Autobiographical bestseller.
