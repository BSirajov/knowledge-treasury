Confucius About

Life and career

Confucius (551–479 BCE) is one of the most influential thinkers in Chinese civilization. His teachings rest on ideals of family order, state ethics, and social harmony.

Scholarly and creative work

Confucianism systematizes ethical principles, ritual, and leadership recorded in sources such as the Analects (Lunyu). These ideas shaped Chinese governance and education for millennia.

Contribution to society

Confucius is among the central figures in world philosophy and ethics; Confucian values remain integral to East Asian cultural identity.

Key works and contributions

  • 1
    Analects (Lunyu)
    Sayings and teachings of Confucius recorded by his disciples.
  • 2
    Confucian ethics
    Principles of ren (humanity), li (ritual), and zhengming (rectification of names).
  • 3
    Educational teaching
    Ideas of philosophical education and self-cultivation.
